Electric Vehicle

An EV is a shortened acronym for an electric vehicle. EVs are vehicles that are either partially or fully powered on electric power. An EV operates on an electric motor, instead of an internal-combustion engine that generates power by burning a mix of fuel and gases. Therefore, such as vehicle is seen as a possible replacement for current-generation automobile, in order to address the issue of rising pollution, global warming, depleting natural resources, etc.

The standard for modern battery electric vehicles is now considered to be lithium ion batteries as they have a greater longevity and are excellent at retaining energy, with a self discharge rate of just 5% per month.

Types of Electric Vehicles

A brief overview of option
Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs)

Powered solely by an electric battery, with no gas engine parts. Most BEVs are capable of fast charging and L2 charging. Zero emissions.
Learn more

Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEVs)

Low-emission vehicles that use an electric motor to assist gas-powered engines. All energy comes from gasoline. Cannot charge with EVgo.
Learn more

Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs)

Similar to a Hybrid, but with a larger battery and electric motor. Has a gas tank and a charging port. Can charge by using L2 chargers.
Learn more

Fuel Cell Electric Vehicle(FCEV)

FCEVs are also known as Zero-Emission Vehicles. They employ ‘fuel cell technology’ to generate the electricity required to run the vehicle.
Learn more
